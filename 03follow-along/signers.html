Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, later the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence, later the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath, inventor, diplomat, and one of the most respected figures of the Revolutionary Era.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress, known for his bold and iconic signature.
Samuel Adams
A key figure in organizing resistance to British rule and a strong advocate for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
The Virginian who proposed the resolution for independence to the Continental Congress.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four major founding documents: the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Association, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson was an American Founding Father, the principal author of the Declaration of Independence, and the third President of the United States. A champion of individual liberty and democratic ideals, he played a key role in shaping the early U.S. government.
John Adams
John Adams was an American Founding Father, the second President of the United States, and a key advocate for independence. Known for his sharp intellect and commitment to justice, he played a crucial role in drafting the Declaration of Independence and negotiating the Treaty of Paris, which ended the Revolutionary War.

These men, along with the other 50 signers, were pivotal in shaping the future of the United States.
